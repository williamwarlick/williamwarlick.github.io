Memories to last a Life Time

In Maine we are fortunate to have an abundance of fishing opportunities with many species of fish and hundreds of lakes, ponds, rivers and streams. Most of my trips are on Sebago Lake targeting the abundant lake trout with fish averaging 3- 6 pounds with an occasional bruiser in teens to 20-pound range. (the biggest on my boat being 23 pounds).

While the landlocked salmon fishing isn’t what it was years ago it is still known as “the home of the landlocked salmon”, one of 4 lakes in the state with native landlocked salmon and is the crown jewel of Sebago lake with fish being netted over 9 pounds. We also have some great local waters for brown trout, splake, largemouth and smallmouth bass as well as pike, crappie and other panfish.

Your Captain

I am Captain Glen Gisel, a Registered Maine Guide and US Coast Guard master captain, with endorsements in freshwater fishing, tidewater fishing, and recreational guiding. I have been fishing on Sebago Lake + the surrounding area for over 40 years, and have been taking people fishing for over 20 years.

My guiding career kind of started when I was 16 years old when during a bass tournament on the lake I lived. One of the anglers saw me catching some monsters from shore and asked me to join him on his boat, with my knowledge of the lake he won the tournament and that’s when it became my dream to be a fishing guide. 40 years later, here I am living that dream! I feel blessed to be able to share that dream with hundreds of people from all over the world every year.

It is my goal to provide you a safe, relaxing, and enjoyable trip on some of Maines most beautiful waters. Consider it fishing therapy, a time to step offshore and leave the hustle and stress of life behind.
