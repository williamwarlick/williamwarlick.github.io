Step off Land...

It's time to take a break and immerse yourself in the tranquility of Maine Waters. As a lifelong fisherman with over four decades of experience on Sebago and the surrounding waters, I know firsthand the healing power of wetting a line and letting go of life's worries.

Planning a fishing trip can be daunting, especially if you're new to the area or lacking the right equipment. That's where Sebago Sport Fishing comes in. As a registered Maine Guide, I'm here to take care of all the details, so you can focus on what matters most: unwinding and creating unforgettable memories on the water.

I've got you covered whether you're seeking a half-day escape, a full-day adventure, or a magical sunset cruise. With top-notch gear and a wealth of local knowledge, I'll guide you to the best fishing spots and help you reel in the catch of a lifetime. And if you're new to fishing, don't worry – I love sharing my expertise and teaching the tricks of the trade.

Fishing Therapy with a Local.

Captain Glen Gisel is a Registered Maine Guide and US Coast Guard master captain. He have been fishing on Sebago Lake + the surrounding area for over 40 years, and have been taking people fishing for over 20 years.

My guiding career started when I was during a bass tournament when I 16 years old. One of the anglers saw me catching some monsters from shore and asked me to join him , with my knowledge of the lake he won the tournament! Since then, it's been my dream to be a fishing guide. 40 years later, here I am living that dream! I feel blessed to be able to share that dream with hundreds of people from all over the world every year.

FAQs

We love furry friends, but for the safety and comfort of all passengers, pets are not allowed on our boats. Service animals are an exception and are welcome onboard.

Alcoholic beverages are permitted onboard for passengers of legal drinking age. However, we encourage responsible consumption and reserve the right to limit or restrict alcohol use if necessary.

We recommend bringing the following items:

  • Sunscreen and hat for sun protection
  • Sunglasses and eyewear retainers
  • Light jacket or windbreaker
  • Comfortable, non-skid footwear
  • Camera or phone for capturing memories
  • Personal medications if needed

My office is at 7 Main Street in Raymond Maine. I keep my boat, Wickedfishy, across the street at Port Harbor Marine -- 1328 Roosevelt Trail in Raymond. AtheneRose stays on trailer ready to be taken anywhere in the Lakes Region.

Safety is our top priority. In case of severe weather conditions that pose a risk to our passengers, we may need to reschedule or cancel the trip. We monitor weather forecasts closely and will contact you in advance if any changes are necessary.
