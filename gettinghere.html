Getting Here

Sebago Lake is easily accessible from Portland, reach Sebago Lake by following these directions:

From Portland:

  1. Head northwest on I-295 N/US-1 N.
  2. Take exit 15 for I-95 N toward Augusta/Gray.
  3. Follow I-95 N for approximately 14 miles.
  4. Take exit 63 for US-202 E/ME-115 E toward Gray/Windham.
  5. Turn right onto US-202 E/ME-115 E and continue for about 4 miles.
  6. Turn left onto ME-35 N and drive for approximately 7 miles.
  7. Sebago Lake will be on your right.

Lodging and Dining

Local dining, places to stay and things to do If you are visiting Maine there are a few things I recommend doing while you're in town...The Maine Wildlife Park is about 20 minutes away from the marina and I highly recommend spending some time there, especially if you have kids. North Conway New Hampshire is about an hour away, it is a very scenic town with a lot of unique shops and inns, while you're there take a drive up Mount Washington and enjoy a spectacular 360 degree view of New England, the highest windspeed ever recorded on planet earth was on Mount Washington. The Casco Bay Lines mail boat cruise is very popular and a great way to see the islands and scenery of Casco Bay and Portland from the ocean and when you get back you can take walk down Commercial Street and get a look at a real-life working waterfront or stroll through the Old port and check out some of the small restaurants, bars, breweries and unique gift shops and maybe finish out the day with a concert or show at Merril Auditorium. Maine has the most lighthouses of any state on the east coast with 65, planning a trip along the coast to see some of them is a great way to experience the Maine coast from New Hampshire to Canada. Portland Headlight is the most photographed lighthouse in America and for good reason, next to Portland Headlight is Fort Williams Park which has some stunning ocean views, beautiful walking and some historic structures. Freeport is the home of LL Beans and about a 45-minute drive, also home to some great restaurants, gift shops and one of the best oyster bars in Maine, the Freeport Oyster Bar. A bit further up the coast is Acadia National Park, it is one of the most beautiful places in the world and if you make arrangements to go up Cadillac Mountain, you'll get to see the sun rise before anyone else in the United States. Another great way to spend a day is to hop on the Downeaster high speed train in Portland or Freeport and take a spin down to Boston and visit The Science Museum, Boston Aquarium, The Freedom Trail, Boston Market and dozens of other attractions.

Local Eateries

During the summer there many great places to eat in the Sebago region, these are some local eateries that I recommend, there are afew ore as you get away from the lake and I can recommend them depending on your travel plans. Kelly's Sebago Diner, Chutes and Top of The Hill Grill are my favorites for breakfast and lunch. For dinner you can't go wrong with any of these..... Rustlers, The Stockhouse, Rose's Italian Restaurant, Bobs Seafood, The Fishermans Catch, Yolked Farm to Table, Captain Jacks, The Freedom Café, The Sportsman Kitchen and Keg in Standish has a dock and you can get there by boat The Beacon is a great local Pizza and sandwich shop. The good Life Market is a wonderful little store with a little bit of everything including a great craft beer and wine selection and very close to the marina. The Village Kitchen is also close to the Marina and open very early for donuts, breakfast sandwiches and coffee. For the sweet tooth, The Mosquito Icecream stand is across the street from the marina and The Icecream Dugout is down the road a mile or so
