What to Expect

Out of professional courtesy to my clients, I do not answer the phone while I am on a guided trip. So if I don’t answer your call please shoot me a text message or email and I will respond to let you know I received it and get back to you after the trip is over.

My calendar changes daily so the first step is to see if I have the date or dates you interested in available, you can call or text me at 207-329-2505, send me an email at glen@sebagosportfishing.com.

If I have the dates available, please fill out the guest registration and hold harmless agreement on this website and email it back to me, this is all that is needed to book a trip and get you locked into the calendar, I do not take deposits.If you have any questions please feel free to call or message me, otherwise I will reach out to you a few days before the trip to confirm and work out any last minute details.

Required at time of trip:

  • A valid Maine Fishing License is required for anyone over 16(purchase it here)
  • A completed Hold Harmless agreement is required. You can download a pdf by clicking here and bring a copy the day of trip. Or fill out and submit the form online in advance.

For the best experience, bring these items:

  • Sun glasses & sun screen
  • Jacket/sweatshirt/rain jacket (depending on weather)
  • Any medicine you might need for the day
  • Camera
  • Personal water bottle

Policies

  • As a registered Maine Guide we are considered an extension of the Maine Warden Service and we are held to a higher standard for following all fish and game laws in the state of Maine and to the best of my knowledge I strictly follow those laws.
  • Unfotantley, no Dogs are allowed on my boat for the danger of a dog being hooked.
  • Any form of drugs including marijuana/CBD is strictly forbidden on my boats, as a US Coast Guard licensed captain, I am subject to federal laws and am at risk of losing that license and getting into all kinds of trouble if they are found on my boats.
  • Alcohol is aloud on my boats, but if you’re looking to party and get drunk, I’m not your guide, I encourage everyone to relax and enjoy themselves with a few beers or a bottle of wine but I reserve the right to end the trip if a client gets out of hand without a refund.
  • Kids under the age of 11 years old are required to wear life jackets on the boat at all times unless we are at anchor, I do have youth life jackets but if they have one you know they are comfortable in please bring it along.
  • By Maine law I cannot legally fillet or remove the head or tail or alter the length of trout, salmon and a few other species, I can clean out the insides though and happy to do it at the end of the trip.
